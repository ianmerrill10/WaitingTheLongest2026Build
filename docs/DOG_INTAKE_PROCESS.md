# WaitingTheLongest.com — Dog Intake Process
## Complete Operational Guide for Shelters, Rescues & Individuals

---

## Overview

WaitingTheLongest.com is a dog adoption discovery platform that ranks dogs exclusively by how long they have been waiting in a shelter. **All listings are submitted by verified organizations or individuals** — we do not scrape other platforms or purchase data.

This document covers the complete intake process from submission through approval through the dog going live on the site.

---

## Who Can Submit

### Tier 0 — Individual / Self-Asserted
**Who:** Individuals fostering a dog, small informal rescues without formal registration
**Verification:** Email address, name, attestation checkbox
**Approval time:** 48-72 hours (manual review)
**Limits:** Max 3 submissions per account until Tier 1

### Tier 1 — Registered Organization (EIN-Verified)
**Who:** Registered 501(c)(3) rescues, shelters with EIN, animal control agencies
**Verification:** EIN number, organization website, contact name and email
**Approval time:** 24 hours
**Limits:** Unlimited submissions, verified badge on all listings

### Tier 2 — Trusted Partner
**Who:** Organizations with established track record on the platform (6+ months, 10+ successful listings)
**Verification:** Automatic upgrade after meeting criteria
**Benefits:** Instant publish (no manual review), featured placement eligible, API access for bulk uploads

## The Submission Flow (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Go to the Intake Portal
URL: **waitingthelongest.com/submit**

The portal is a 5-step form accessible from any device. No account required for Tier 0 submissions.

---

### Step 2: Identify Yourself

Select your submitter type:
- **Individual** — you personally have custody of the dog (foster, owner surrender situation)
- **Rescue / Foster Network** — informal or registered rescue organization
- **Shelter / Animal Control** — official shelter or municipal animal control

Fill in:
- Your full name
- Email address
- Phone number (optional but recommended for follow-up)
- Social media handle(s) (helps with verification for Tier 0)
- For Tier 1: Organization name, EIN, website

---

### Step 3: Dog Details

These fields power the search, filtering, and wait counter on the site:

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Dog's name | Yes | The name they use at the shelter |
| **Intake date** | **YES — CRITICAL** | The date the dog physically entered the shelter/foster care. This is the source of the live wait counter. Use the REAL date, not today's date. |
| Primary breed | Yes | Be honest. "Pit Bull Mix" is fine. Don't list as "Lab Mix" if it's clearly a pit bull — it erodes trust. |
| Secondary breed | No | Optional for mixed-breed dogs |
| Is mixed breed | Yes | Checkbox |
| Age | Yes | Months (under 12 months) or years |
| Size | Yes | Small (<20 lbs), Medium (20-50 lbs), Large (50-90 lbs), XL (90+ lbs) |
| Sex | Yes | Male / Female |
| Spayed/Neutered | Yes | Yes / No / Unknown |
| Description | Recommended | 100-500 chars. What makes this dog special? What happened to them? Write it like you're telling a friend. |
| Good with dogs | Recommended | Yes / No / Unknown |
| Good with cats | Recommended | Yes / No / Unknown |
| Good with kids | Recommended | Yes / No / Unknown |
| Special needs | Yes | Check if dog has medical or behavioral special needs |
| Location (city, state) | Yes | Where the dog physically is |
| Listing URL | Recommended | Direct link to the dog's profile on your organization's site or Chewy/Adopt-a-Pet listing |

**About the intake date:** This is the most important field. The wait counter displayed on the dog's live profile page ticks up from this date in real time. If a dog entered your shelter on January 3rd, 2025, use January 3rd, 2025 — even if you're submitting the dog to us today in June 2026. The counter reflects the actual time waiting, not the time since listing with us.

---

### Step 4: Photos

- **Minimum:** 1 photo
- **Maximum:** 5 photos
- **Format:** JPG, PNG, WebP
- **Max file size:** 10 MB per photo
- **First photo = primary photo** — appears in all search results and social sharing

**Photo tips that actually increase adoption rates:**
- Natural light. Outside or near a window. No kennel backgrounds if avoidable.
- Get down to their eye level. Don't shoot from above.
- A toy or treat in front of their nose gets bright, alert eyes.
- Multiple photos showing personality: resting, playing, close-up of face.
- If the dog is black-coated: bright natural light is critical. Dark backgrounds disappear them. Use a light wall or outdoor grass background.

---

### Step 5: Legal Agreement

Before submitting, you must agree to three things:

1. **Accuracy attestation:** All information submitted is accurate and complete. False information about a dog's history, temperament, or status constitutes fraud and may result in civil and criminal liability.

2. **Rights grant:** You have legal authority to post this dog. You are not a commercial breeder, pet store, or broker. No for-profit dog sales are permitted on WaitingTheLongest.com — violators are reported to the appropriate authorities.

3. **Content license:** You grant WaitingTheLongest.com an unlimited, royalty-free license to display, distribute, and use your submitted photos and descriptions for the purpose of finding the dog a home. This includes social media, email, and marketing materials.

Full terms available at: waitingthelongest.com/terms/submission

## What Happens After Submission

### Confirmation
- You receive a confirmation email immediately with your submission ID.
- The dog is NOT yet live on the site.

### Review Process
Every submission is reviewed by a human before going live. We check:
- Intake date is plausible (not in the future, not obviously fabricated)
- Photos meet minimum quality standards
- Description doesn't contain prohibited content (price lists, breeder language, contact info for direct sales)
- Submitter contact information is valid
- No signs of puppy mill, broker, or commercial operation

### Approval Notification
- You receive an email when the dog goes live, with a direct link to their profile page.
- The live wait counter will immediately reflect their full wait time from the intake date.

### Rejection Notification
- If rejected, you receive an email explaining why and how to resubmit correctly.
- Common rejection reasons: intake date missing or clearly wrong, no photos, commercial breeder language in description.

## After the Dog Is Listed

### Sharing the Listing
Every dog's profile page has:
- **ShareKit** — pre-written TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter/X share text with the dog's name, breed, and wait time
- **Direct URL** — `waitingthelongest.com/dogs/[id]` — shareable anywhere
- **Live wait counter** — the seconds ticking up in real time make social shares emotionally compelling

### When a Dog Gets Adopted
Email us at shelters@waitingthelongest.com with:
- Dog's name
- Your organization name
- (Optional) Brief note about the adoption — we may feature their success story on the blog

We will update the listing status to "Adopted" and archive the profile. Adopted dogs are celebrated, not deleted.

### Updating a Listing
To update information (changed location, new photos, updated description):
Email shelters@waitingthelongest.com with the dog's name and what needs to change.

Self-service listing management is on the roadmap for Tier 1+ organizations.

## Data Standards (SAC-Aligned)

WaitingTheLongest.com uses vocabulary aligned with the [Shelter Animals Count (SAC)](https://www.shelteranimalscount.org/) national database standard. This means:

- **Intake types:** Stray, Owner Surrender, Transfer, Return, Confiscation, Born in Care
- **Size categories:** Small, Medium, Large, XL
- **Age groupings:** Puppy (0-6mo), Young (6-18mo), Adult (18mo-7yr), Senior (7yr+)
- **Altered status:** Yes, No, Unknown

Using standard vocabulary ensures your data integrates cleanly and allows accurate national benchmarking.

## Prohibited Submissions (Zero Tolerance)

The following submissions are permanently banned:

1. **Commercial dog sales** — Any dog listed with a price (adoption fee is fine; sale price is not), breeder information, or "available puppies" language
2. **Puppy mills / brokers** — Any submission from a commercial breeding operation or pet broker
3. **Impersonation** — Claiming to be an organization you are not
4. **Fabricated wait times** — Using false intake dates to artificially inflate wait time rankings
5. **Dogs not in your custody** — Submitting dogs that are not in your physical care or your organization's care

Violations are reported to the Humane Society, ASPCA, and local law enforcement where applicable. No warnings — permanent ban on first violation.
